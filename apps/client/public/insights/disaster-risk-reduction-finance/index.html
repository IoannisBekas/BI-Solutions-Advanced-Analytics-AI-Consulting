Finance context and donors

Does international finance match risk?

OECD CRS is reported international DRR-related ODA. World Bank and GCF figures are project finance signals, while OCHA FTS is response funding context. CRS figures use the latest detailed donor-recipient-sector year available to the pipeline; preliminary aggregate ODA releases are not used for country donor breakdowns. Domestic, private, and mainstreamed DRR remain separate evidence tiers.

FAQ

Questions people ask about DRR data

Short answers for researchers, NGOs, donors, and policy teams evaluating whether this atlas fits their country-screening workflow.

Where can I find country finance context?

The atlas provides country-level finance context, including OECD CRS DRR-related ODA, World Bank DRM-tagged project signals, Green Climate Fund resilience project signals, OCHA FTS humanitarian response context, World Bank WDI population/GDP denominators, and GDACS current-alert context where available. OECD CRS figures use the latest detailed donor-recipient-sector year available to the pipeline; preliminary aggregate ODA releases are treated as context, not as replacements for country donor breakdowns.

Which countries have high disaster risk and low readiness?

Use the Risk, Country, Compare, and Finance views to screen countries by INFORM Risk, WorldRiskIndex, ND-GAIN readiness, disaster loss, displacement, and reported international support indicators.

What is the difference between INFORM Risk and the Sendai Framework Monitor?

INFORM Risk is a composite risk index for humanitarian crises and disasters. The Sendai Framework Monitor tracks official government reporting against Sendai Framework targets. This atlas brings selected public signals into one country-level view with source-linked metrics for traceability.

Which public source systems are included?

The atlas includes public data from Our World in Data, OCHA FTS, OECD CRS, World Bank Projects and Operations, World Bank WDI, GCF open data, GDACS, INFORM, EM-DAT, ND-GAIN, WorldRiskIndex, WRI Aqueduct, and ThinkHazard. Metric coverage follows the source audit.

Can I compare reported support per person or against GDP?

Yes. The country, finance, compare, and export views include World Bank WDI population and GDP denominators so users can compare DRR-related ODA per person and as a share of GDP where source values are available.

Does the atlas show current disaster alerts?

Yes. GDACS current alerts are shown as operational context by country, including alert count, alert level, event type, latest alert title, and affected-population metadata where available. These alerts are not treated as historical loss records.

Can NGOs and organizations use this atlas for proposal screening or country risk analysis?

Yes. It supports country screening, proposal scoping, donor mapping, and risk-finance comparison, with source references available for due diligence and follow-up analysis.
